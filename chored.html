App Overview

Chore'd is an Android app designed for managing chores or tasks within different groups.

With this app, users can create and join groups and manage tasks within each group. They can add tasks for next week and volunteer for next week's tasks before the week starts. On Sunday, which is when the week begins, an algorithm allocates the remaining chores that weren't volunteered for to the members of the group.

Members of a group can see the entire list of chores for the week on the group page, which also shows which tasks have already been completed (the tasks would be crossed out once completed). They can also see whose responsible, allowing them to remind the person to finish their chore.

With Chore'd, users are organized and on top of their weekly tasks.

Further Changes

After showing our customer team the prototypes I created, we basically went straight into coding on Android Studio. This is because the class wasn't focused on HCI or UI/UX design and more emphasized on the software engineering and technical aspects of the app.

For each week, we had a certain part of the app we focused on. For example, the first week of coding we focused on the login page and the functionality behind it. I don't have screenshots of changes that we made on Android Studio, but the login page evolved from just a blank screen to having 2 input fields for "Username" and "Password" and just 2 buttons ("Login" and "Register"). After that, we presented this iteration to our customer team and received feedback. They wanted to lighten the fields for the username and password, so we changed that for our next iteration, as well as adding the logo. The "Reset Password" button came later as our customer team decided they wanted that feature in the later weeks.

That's basically how each week would go: focus on one major aspect of the app, receive feedback, then make changes for the upcoming iteration and build upon it/add new features. (Please check out our user stories to see the progress we made throughout the quarter.)

O V E R V I E W

This class allowed us to develop Android apps and learn about common design patterns seen in the industry. Our class was set up so that we were assigned to be the customer team of one group and the developer team for a different group. We had a weekly lab session to meet up with our customer and developer teams to show them what we accomplished for that week's iteration. By going through and experiencing the agile method, I learned the importance of milestones and iterations. For our team, we were asked to develop a chores management app.

I had no experience with Android Studio, so I learned a lot by the end of the quarter. I had the role of UI/UX designer, so I was responsible for designing the user interfaces and layouts of the different screens and pages for the app. The customer team had many ideas they wanted us to incorporate into the final product, various ones being design ideas, such as adding the sidebar, so we worked with them to develop their ideas. I also took part in helping with the front-end development by fixing bugs I stumbled across when I went through possible user scenarios.

This project gave me an idea of how the industry works and through the this class, I learned how to work in customer/developer teams. It was definitely more enjoyable than a regular lecture-based class and I hope to continue developing more skills in regards to app development.
