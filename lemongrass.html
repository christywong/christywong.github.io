Project Overview

Lemongrass is a restaurant located at UC San Diego's Price Center. A business that originally started as a stand at farmers' markets around San Diego and at UCSD's weekly farmers' market was invited a permanent spot at UCSD due to its overwhelming popularity with the students.

We offered Pat, the owner, a website redesign; something that kept Lemongrass's organic & natural branding, yet was also youthful & fun.

Homepage

What We Found

From our user research, the old website's homepage was
    • uncaptivating
    • boring
    • glitchy
    • text-heavy
    • not modern
    This created an overall unpleasant first impression.

    What We Did

    For our redesign, we wanted the home page to be visually appealing, inviting, and representative of what Lemongrass is.

    Thus, we...
    • Used their signature dish as their header
    • Condendesed their hours of operation
    • Scrapped the Directions page & opted for text for the location because most customers are UCSD students & staff who know where Price Center is
    • Included a gallery slider for announcements (owner is coming out with new dishes)
    • Created an About page for the text-heavy description

    Menu

    What We Found

    The old website's menu was
      • text-heavy
      • not updated
      • lacking in visuals
      • unclear
      The menu was the most difficult to redesign because there were many dishes and categories. We wanted to showcase every dish visually, but found that if we put all the dishes on a single page, it was overwhelming for the user to look at.

      What We Did

      • Created galleries/sliders for each major category of the menu (can slide through options quickly on mobile)
      • Descriptions on the gallery photo (desktop) and below image (mobile) for easy readibility
      • Included a key at the top for icons used
      • Removed dishes that weren't offered anymore
      • Included a banner for a catering option (because no one knew if this was offered on the old site)
      • Included links to download their app (once again, no one knew this existed)

      About

      What We Found

      The text-heavy paragraph on the old website's homepage was distracting. Users didn't like it and didn't want to read it. However, we still wanted to include something about the owner and her business, because it's an inspiring story.

      What We Did

      Thus, we created a separate page for users to read more about Pat, the owner, as well as Lemongrass's background and how it came to be what it is today. We put photos between sections of text to not overwhelm the reader with words.

      Get in Touch

      What We Found

      Users said they would unlikely use the old website's contact page and preferred using Yelp for reviews.

      What We Did

      However, the owner likes getting feedback and suggestions from customers, so we decided to keep this on the site (just as another option for users to write something to Lemongrass). It's just a simple contact form that's easy to use.
