Problem

Not all courses offer online resources, such as Piazza, for students to post questions and discuss homework. Not all students know their classmates. So where do these students go to for help? Who do these students ask if they don't know anyone in their class?

Solution

StudyBuddies, a web-based application, allows UCSD students to create and join study groups for the courses they are taking. Through this, students can meet new people, help one another academically, and develop better study habits.

F E A T U R E S

My Classes
Available study groups for your classes are shown, allowing users to conveniently join or leave groups
My Groups
Users can edit the study groups they have created and view the ones they joined, all organized by date
Create a Study Group
Users can easily create their own study groups
Additional Study Resources
Users can not only find links to available study resources, but also submit their own and rate each resource on their helpfulness

O V E R V I E W

Coming into this class with absolutely no prior knowledge of HTML, CSS, and JavaScript made it a bit difficult. I didn't even know what prototyping tools were! However, I learned a lot and am proud to have contributed to this project.

Throughout the course of developing this website, I learned the basics of HTML5, CSS3, and Javascript, as well as using prototyping tools, such as Justinmind. The first few weeks of class were spent not only on brainstorming and prototyping our potential features and ideas, but also learning about user experience. We received user feedback from fellow classmates and took their suggestions into consideration as we progressed with our designs.

I designed the logo for our web app, and had a role in helping design the user interface, as well as coding the study resources page. This was my first time building a website (from scratch, nonetheless!), so there was a bit of a steep learning curve. However, as coding always is, the end result is definitely worth it. I had always been interested in web design and this course and project just furthered my interest. This was also the first time I had exposure to any HCI and UX design, and I absolutely fell in love with it. Thinking about the end-user's perspective is what many computer science classes lack, and I was amazed that classes don't emphasize that viewpoint, especially since the user is such a critical factor in any project. But thanks to this course, I was exposed to a different point of view and have found a field I would love to pursue.
